Building a personal space on the web is rarely a solitary endeavour, and indeed this website is no exception. From the very beginning, it has been built upon the open-source contributions, creative research, and shared knowledge of many others. The content you read here is a reflection of the people and their tools that have enabled me to document my work and experiment with new ideas, and to them I am very grateful.

I am grateful firstly to [Jacky Zhao](https://jzhao.xyz/) for developing [Quartz](https://quartz.jzhao.xyz/). This website simply would not exist without his commitment to creating democratic, open-source publishing tools. The root of this site lies in [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/), and I owe a debt of gratitude to their team for building an environment that makes organising complex thoughts feel intuitive and offering it to the general public completely free of charge. Furthermore, I would like to thank the [p5.js Association](https://p5js.org/about/) for their tireless work in making creative coding accessible to people such as myself, to whom it would have otherwise been impossible.

The interactive elements of this site were heavily influenced by educators who dedicate their time to making complex algorithms approachable. I would like to thank the amazing [Daniel Shiffman](https://shiffman.net/), whose [Coding Train](https://thecodingtrain.com/) tutorials provided the original spark for many of the animations found here and gave me the confidence to try to make my own. Also in this vein is the very talented [Patt Vira](https://www.pattvira.com/), whom I thank for her insightful p5.js videos and creative coding ideas.
